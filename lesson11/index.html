Lobs of Fun Festival - 2021

by Megan A. Wirsch

Was it cloudy with a chance of meatballs over the weekend? No, not a 'chance'. So many meatballs, gallons of sauce, and pasta noodles fell on participants of the great Idaho "Lobs of Fun Festival". Traditionally held as a water balloon fight, organizers considered cancelling the festival as the regional drought persisted and water conservation measures were implemented. Thankfully the Noodle Caboodle Wholesale Warehouse offered a yummy alternative.


Now being referred to as the "world's biggest food fight", the events began with the unloading of 18 tons of pasta, sauce, and delicious meatballs. Over 3600 people participated in the scrumptious battle by hurling meatballs and saucy pasta globs. With tickets going for $8 a pop, the organizers raised nearly $30,000 for local farmers hit hardest by the drought.


While pulling strings of spaghetti from her hair, Karen Anderson reported, "I've always wanted to be in a food fight. It was so great--truly a once-in-a-lifetime experience." People of all ages participated, with the oldest food-fighter being reported as celebrating her 97th birthday while covered in sauce.
